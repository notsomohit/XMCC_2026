About XIM University

XIM University, Bhubaneswar, embodies the philosophy of “Inspiring Futures” ; nurturing young minds to innovate, lead, and pursue new paths. Committed to societal progress and sustainable development, the university serves as a catalyst for change across Odisha and India. It offers diverse programs in Management, Law, Rural and Sustainability Studies, Communications, Engineering, Economics, Humanities, Compassion Studies, and Governance, preparing visionary and values-driven leaders. The experiential “learning by doing” model, supported by active student forums, fosters innovation, leadership, and personal growth. A vibrant campus life enriched by sports, cultural activities, and social engagement complements academic excellence. Guided by its mission of justice and human dignity, the university supports marginalized communities, promotes ethical leadership, and champions environmental stewardship.

About Xavier Law School

Xavier Law School, XIM University, is a leading new-age law institution recognized by the Bar Council of India. It is equipped with advanced physical, digital, and academic infrastructure, and is set amidst the greenery of suburban Bhubaneswar. The school is dedicated to shaping socially responsible and ethically grounded legal professionals. Its academic strength is founded on rigorous teaching, research, and practice-oriented learning. Students receive comprehensive professional training through a digitally enabled Moot Court Hall, an active Legal Aid Clinic, and access to premier databases like SCC Online, Manupatra, and Lexis Advance. The school also supports diverse learners with a well-curated library and inclusive scholarship programs. Student life is enhanced by four active committees—Moot Court, Legal Aid, ADR, and Literary & Cultural—which cultivate leadership and community engagement. Dedicated training and placement support prepare students for careers in law firms, corporations, PSUs, chambers, and other legal sectors.

About the Moot Court Committee

The Moot Court Committee (MCC) at Xavier Law School provides a dynamic platform for students to build advocacy skills, refine legal reasoning, and participate in realistic courtroom simulations. The goal is to ensure every student gains substantial mooting experience before entering professional practice, strengthening their research, drafting, oral advocacy, and courtroom etiquette. The MCC cultivates a strong mooting culture through structured competitions, including the inaugural National Moot Court Competition held in collaboration with the National Human Rights Commission, and regular intra-semester moots judged by experts. Workshops and training sessions further enhance students’ advocacy and research capabilities, preparing them for national and international legal arenas.

About the Xavier Moot Court Competition (XMCC)

The Xavier Moot Court Competition (XMCC) is the flagship annual event of the Moot Court Committee at Xavier Law School, XIM University. Recognized for its academic rigor and high standards of advocacy, XMCC has established itself as one of India’s distinguished national moot court competitions. Its growth is marked by notable collaborations, including an edition with the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) which earned national recognition for its substantive human-rights discourse. A subsequent edition was held in collaboration with the Competition Commission of India (CCI), introducing advanced issues in competition law. Most recently, XMCC partnered with the ICSI–IIP, an edition acclaimed for its strong administration and expert judging panels. Each year, XMCC offers a valuable platform for experiential learning and professional development, bringing together exceptional student advocates, carefully crafted legal propositions, and distinguished judges. Announcement: Xavier Law School is pleased to announce that this year’s competition will be organized in collaboration with the Central Detective Training Institute (CDTI), Jaipur, further strengthening the competition’s tradition of excellence and academic distinction.
